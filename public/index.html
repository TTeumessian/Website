Security in The Age of AGI

AI and autonomous agents are not the future; they are the present. Autonomous systems are rapidly transforming healthcare, education, media, governance, and nearly every other domain. Their influence is growing at an exponential rate, soon permeating every corner of the economy and world. But this transformation comes at a cost: an expanding, novel, and highly complex attack surface whose security is lagging dangerously far behind the pace of innovation. Misaligned military agents could compromise national defense. Comprimised trading agentic could manipulate and destabilize public markets. Unsecure LLMs could leak sensitive information. Rogue social media agents could spread disinformation at an unprecedented scale, reshaping public perception. These vast risks are largely unexplored and these systems poorly protected. Like all technology, AI and agents provide leverage; leverage that will drive unparalleled economic and technological progress and prosperity. However, if they are compromised, that leverage can and will be turned against us by threat actors to catastrophic outcomes. In this emerging era, corporations and nations face a choice: secure these systems proactively or become victims of their own creation.
