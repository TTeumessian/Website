Security in The Intelligence Age

Agentic AI, which are autonomous systems equipped with tools to achieve specific goals, are not the future; they are the present. Agents are rapidly transforming healthcare, education, media, governance, and nearly every other domain. Their influence will soon permeating every corner of the digital, and eventually physical, world. But this transformation comes at a cost: an expanding, novel, and highly complex attack surface whose security is lagging dangerously far behind the pace of innovation. Compromised autonomous military systems could cripple national defense. Misaligned trading agents could manipulate and destabilize public markets. Insecure LLMs could leak sensitive or dangerous information. Rogue social media agents could spread disinformation at an unprecedented scale. Hijacked automatons powered by AI could be weaponized against their users. These vast risks are underexplored, and these systems remain poorly protected against them. While autonomous AI systems offer unparalleled leverage to drive economic, scientific, and technological progress, if compromised, that same leverage can be turned against us with disastrous consequences. In this emerging era, corporations and nations face a choice: secure these systems proactively or risk becoming victims of their own creation.
